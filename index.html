Fragment-based quantum chemistry for complex molecular and materials systems

The XU-QCgroup develops quantum chemistry methods, molecular simulation workflows, and scientific software for studying complex chemical, biological, and materials systems. Our central goal is to extend accurate quantum-mechanical modeling to systems that are too large, heterogeneous, or electronically complex for conventional approaches.

Research

We focus on fragment-based quantum chemistry, embedding theory, automated computational workflows, and applications in molecular recognition, bioinorganic chemistry, and materials modeling.

Method development

We develop effective fragment potential, effective fragment molecular orbital, and QM/EFP methods, with an emphasis on open-shell, multireference, and scalable formulations for complex electronic environments.

Workflows and software

We build automated workflows and user-friendly interfaces that make advanced fragmentation methods easier to use in high-throughput quantum chemistry and molecular simulation studies.

Applications

Current applications include metal–peptide coordination, protein–ligand binding, molecular recognition, solvation, and emerging problems in polymeric and nanoscale materials.
